## Design Context

### Users
Health-conscious adults who track hydration as part of a broader wellness routine. They use the app multiple times daily — quick-logging water during work, meals, or workouts. They expect a polished, trustworthy experience that feels like a premium health tool, not a toy.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, clean, premium.** The app should feel like a quiet companion — confident without being loud, helpful without being needy. Think refined restraint, not flashy features.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Dark mode only. Deep navy base (`#060B18`) with cerulean blue (`#3B9FE3`) and warm amber (`#F0A050`) accents.
- **Typography:** Poppins across all weights. Thin for hero metrics, SemiBold for structure, Regular for body.
- **Visual tone:** Premium health dashboard — data-forward but not clinical. Every element earns its place.
- **Anti-references:** Avoid gamification aesthetics (streaks, badges, confetti). Avoid illustration-heavy wellness apps. Avoid cluttered data dashboards.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet confidence** — The interface should feel authoritative and polished without demanding attention. No visual noise.
2. **Effortless logging** — The primary interaction (logging water) must be achievable in under 2 seconds. Remove all friction.
3. **Information density without clutter** — Show useful data (progress, goal, weather) but never overwhelm. Progressive disclosure over upfront complexity.
4. **Warm precision** — Balance the technical (data, calculations, metrics) with warmth (encouraging copy, amber accents, generous spacing).
5. **Purposeful restraint** — Every color, animation, and element must have a reason. If it doesn't serve the user, remove it.
